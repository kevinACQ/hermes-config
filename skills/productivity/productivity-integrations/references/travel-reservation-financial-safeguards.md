# Travel Reservation Changes With Financial Safeguards

Use for replacing or canceling hotel/flight reservations where fees, refunds, or a new card charge are possible.

## Safe Sequence

1. **Capture the original reservation:** property/carrier, guest, dates, room/fare type, confirmation number, total, payment status if visible, and cancellation deadline.
2. **Find the replacement from the authoritative source:** preserve dates, occupancy, and guest requirements; do not rely on a paraphrased message when a direct booking link exists.
3. **Price and policy check before cancellation:** verify replacement availability, taxes/fees, cancellation terms, and final total. Prefer a like-for-like room/fare; identify any material downgrade.
4. **Inspect the original cancellation modal:** require explicit no-fee/full-refund language before confirming. Distinguish a refund from release of an authorization or cancellation of an unpaid reservation.
5. **Confirm and verify cancellation:** capture success text plus cancellation number/email receipt. Do not claim the card refund has settled unless the ledger shows the credit.
6. **Prepare the replacement:** fill non-sensitive details when known, let the user's password manager/payment extension handle private payment data, and stop at the final charge button.
7. **Approval checkpoint:** present one compact summary with exact total, dates, room/fare, cancellation policy, and savings/difference. Obtain approval for that exact charge.
8. **After booking:** verify confirmation number, charged total, dates, guest, and cancellation deadline.

## Policy Fallback

If the checkout page omits or hides a cancellation policy, consult the property's official FAQ/policies page or call the property. Save the authoritative wording and do not infer refundability from a rate name such as “Best Available Rate.”

## Duplicate-Button Pitfall

Cancellation pages often retain a background “Cancel” button while displaying a modal with another. Scope the click to the visible modal/form and verify exact fee text immediately before confirmation.

## Reporting Language

Use precise distinctions:
- “Cancellation successful; fee shown as $0” is not automatically “refund settled.”
- “Full refund eligible” is not “credit posted.”
- Quote the final total including taxes, not only the nightly/base rate.
